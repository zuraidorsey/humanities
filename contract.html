About the Contract

The University of Texas at Austin Honors Program provides a contract-based framework for rigorous, personalized academic pursuits. My Honors contract focuses on International Political Economy with a specialized concentration in Artificial Intelligence and Cybersecurity governance.

Rather than merely completing degree requirements, I work with faculty advisors to design an individualized four-year course of study that emphasizes rigorous legal research, comparative policy analysis, and deep engagement with the frameworks governing international technology markets. The contract serves as both a roadmap and a commitment to distinguishing myself in the study of global governance structures.

Key components of my Honors Contract include:

  • Advanced Seminars: Small, discussion-based honors courses emphasizing comparative policy analysis and theoretical frameworks
  • Research Focus: Sustained investigation of AI governance, cybersecurity regulation, and intellectual property law
  • Capstone Thesis: Original research project examining international AI governance regimes and their impact on technology and creative industries
  • Faculty Mentorship: Close collaboration with faculty in law, international relations, and business schools
  • Interdisciplinary Approach: Integration of legal, economic, and policy perspectives across departments

Contract Goals & Outcomes

Through completion of this honors contract, I aim to:

  1. Develop advanced expertise in comparative international AI governance and regulatory frameworks
  2. Analyze the impact of GDPR, CCPA, NIST, ISO 27701, and other compliance standards on global markets
  3. Understand the intersection of intellectual property law and technology innovation
  4. Conduct sustained, original research on international AI governance regimes
  5. Produce a rigorous honors thesis contributing to scholarly understanding of technology policy
  6. Build relationships with faculty mentors in law, international relations, and business
  7. Develop skills in policy analysis, research communication, and professional leadership
  8. Position myself for advanced study or professional work in technology policy and law
